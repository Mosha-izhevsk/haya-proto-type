About Hayani ha zwitori

Stories for gender-based violence awareness and social change

Logo Image

Our Story

Hayani ha zwitori (Home of Stories) was created with a powerful purpose: to raise awareness about gender-based violence through compelling storytelling. We believe that stories can be a catalyst for social change, helping to shed light on difficult issues while fostering empathy and understanding.

Our platform features narratives that explore the realities of gender-based violence, its impact on individuals and communities, and pathways to healing and justice. The stories featured on our website are hand-crafted by Nethononda Mutshidzi, a talented storyteller dedicated to using literature as a tool for social awareness and change.

Through these narratives, we aim to educate, inspire action, and provide support to those affected by gender-based violence. We believe that by bringing these stories to light, we can contribute to breaking the cycle of violence and creating a more just and equitable society.

Our Mission

To use storytelling as a powerful tool for raising awareness about gender-based violence, challenging harmful norms, and promoting social change. We aim to create a platform where these important narratives can reach a wide audience, fostering understanding and inspiring action to address gender-based violence in our communities.

Our Values

  • • Empathy: We believe in the power of stories to foster understanding and compassion.
  • • Advocacy: We are committed to advocating for survivors of gender-based violence.
  • • Education: We aim to educate communities about the realities and impact of gender-based violence.
  • • Empowerment: We seek to empower individuals to speak out and take action against gender-based violence.

Gender-Based Violence Awareness

Gender-based violence (GBV) is a pervasive issue that affects millions of people worldwide. It includes physical, sexual, emotional, and economic abuse, as well as harmful practices rooted in gender inequality. Through our stories, we aim to:

  • • Illustrate the various forms of gender-based violence and their impact on individuals and communities
  • • Challenge harmful cultural norms and practices that perpetuate gender inequality
  • • Highlight pathways to support, healing, and justice for survivors
  • • Promote dialogue and action to prevent gender-based violence

Our stories are crafted with sensitivity and respect, drawing on real-life experiences while protecting the privacy and dignity of survivors. We believe that by bringing these narratives to light, we can contribute to breaking the silence around gender-based violence and fostering a culture of respect, equality, and non-violence.

Featured Work

Book Cover

Vhutshiloni u tshi khou aluwa u si na vhabebi tsini

by Nethononda Mutshidzi

This powerful story follows Maemu, a 14-year-old orphan living with her aunt. Through Maemu's experiences, the narrative explores themes of emotional abuse, neglect, and the vulnerability of children without parental protection. The story highlights how gender-based violence can manifest in various forms, including the mistreatment of vulnerable young girls.

Support Resources

If you or someone you know is experiencing gender-based violence, help is available. Below are some resources that provide support, information, and assistance:

  • • Gender-Based Violence Command Centre: 0800 428 428 or *120*7867# (free from any cell phone)
  • • People Opposing Women Abuse (POWA): 011 642 4345/6
  • • Childline South Africa: 0800 055 555
  • • South African Police Service: 10111

Remember, you are not alone, and reaching out for help is a sign of strength, not weakness.
